KG's karaoke-video-player -- Brief User Guide

Update: 20220301

1. What this app can do

  • Records a video of your practice/performance with playing karaoke audio.
  • Exports the recorded video and recorded karaoke audio to be downloades.
  • Plays the mix of the recorded video and karaoke audio, then exports it.
  • (optionally) Records and exports desktop audio while playing YouTube video or whatever.
  • may use external webcam, audio interface, mics

This app runs on many web browsers including Firefox, Chrome, Safari and hopefully on mobile version of those.

Downloaded JavaScript app is cached and runs offline until it is deleted from the web browsers cache.

Limitations

  • You cannot monitor your voice from headphones. The captured sound from a mic is late by some 10 milliseconds and useless for singing.
  • More than one live audio inputs cannot be used at the same time by browsers capability limitation. Then a karaoke audio file should be prepared before singing.
You may use direct monitoring function of mic input by enabling so called "loop back" or "direct monitoring" on the device.

2. Use cases (mainly about audio input)

Video camera: Built-in or USB webcam. Also wireless webcam may work.

A. Built-in speaker and mic

  • Pros: Simple and easy
  • Cons: Karaoke sound from the speakers is recorded with vocal.

B. Headset (mic + headphones)

Note: Headphones and built-in mic does not work for one hole jack on a laptop PC. The built-in mic will be switched to headset mic.
  • Pros: Simple and easy. No bleed of karaoke to mic.
  • Cons: Vocal may be too loud and distorted unless input gain is properly adjusted by device dependent way.

C. External USB audio input and headphones/earphones

External USB audio includes USB microphone, USB audio interface, USB Webcam, etc. Wireless webcam may work with additional latency.
  • Pros: Best vocal sound quality. No bleed of karaoke to mic.
  • Cons: Rather complex set up required.
Audio output can be switched to an audio interface. Most USB mic nor webcam do not have audio output.

"Stop": Stop recording or playback
"Playback": (optional) Playback recorded audio to check if it is good.
